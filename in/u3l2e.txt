::U3L2Q43::Answer the following questions.<br>Why are fossil fuels like coal considered nonrenewable resources?{=Fossil fuels like coal are considered non-renewable resources because they are formed from the remains of ancient plants and animals that were subjected to intense heat and pressure over millions of years. These resources are finite and cannot be replenished at a rate comparable to their consumption.}

::U3L2Q44::Answer the following questions.<br>What are some pros and cons of using crude oil as an energy source?{=Pros of using crude oil as an energy source include its high energy density, widespread availability and established infrastructure for extraction, transportation and refining. Cons include its non-renewable nature, contribution to greenhouse gas emissions and climate change, environmental risks from spills and air pollution from combustion.}

::U3L2Q45::Answer the following questions.<br>How does hydraulic fracturing enable greater fossil fuel extraction and what are some concerns associated with this practice?{=Hydraulic fracturing, or fracking, involves injecting a high-pressure mixture of water, sand and chemicals into rock formations to create fractures and extract trapped oil and natural gas. It has enabled greater extraction of fossil fuels from previously inaccessible reserves. However, concerns include potential water contamination, induced seismic activity and air pollution from the release of methane and other pollutants.}
